Based on a €9.5bn project cost and 27,876 marginal residents. MetroLink's primary case rests on network connectivity, journey time reduction, and induced demand — not residential catchment alone. This figure is an honest counter-finding and should be contextualised in any public-facing write-up.

Method: Areal-weighted population apportionment from CSO Census 2022 Small Areas (SAPS, column T1_1AGETT). Marginal gain polygon derived by Vector Difference: MetroLink 10-minute walking isochrone (ORS Tools, foot-walking, 4.8 km/h) minus existing DART, Luas Green Line, Luas Red Line, and commuter rail catchment (TFI GTFS, route_type 0 and 2). Bus and BusConnects corridors excluded — "high-capacity rail/LRT" definition applied consistently. Analysis CRS: EPSG:2157 (Irish Transverse Mercator). Study extent: Dublin City, Fingal, South Dublin, Dún Laoghaire-Rathdown. MetroLink alignment based on current Railway Order; subject to change.
